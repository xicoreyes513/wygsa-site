Hispanic Heritage Month

Salice Rose

  • Salice Rose is a bisexual Peruvian American social media influencer. You can find her dedicating her time helping out the community, where just recently she spend $20,000 worth of masks to a children's hospital. However, not only does she connect to her supporters, helping them in any way possible, she also provides them with advice and important messages to encourage a better community.

Holly Woodlawn

  • Holly Woodlawn was a transgender Puerto Rican actress who appears in the movies Trash (1970) and Women in Revolt (1971). Although she had a tough experience adjusting to the real world, she eventually settled with a man she loved, and in a blink of an eye became famous. Her sassy, improvised dialogue and her vulnerability touched audiences making her a well known actress at the time.

Sylvia Rivera

  • Sylvia Rivera was an American gay liberation and transgender rights activist who was also a noted community worker in New York. Rivera, who identified as a drag queen, participated in demonstrations with the Gay Liberation Front. Rivera helped lead the charge on the night of the Stonewall riots in New York City, considered the beginning of the LGBT rights movement.

Dennis Deleon

  • Dennis Deleon (1948-2009) was an American human rights lawyer, HIV/AIDS activist, and a Latino community leader. Throughout his life, DeLeon sought to eliminate the health differences between marginalized communities. His political position allowed deLeon to bring all cultural differences together, where he maintained a concern for people of color, especially those affected by the HIV/AIDS epidemic. Unfortunately, deLeon did die due to HIV that caused heart failure.

Angie Xtravaganza

  • Angie Xtravaganza was a co-founder and Mother of the House of Xtravaganza. A prominent transgender performer in New York City's gay ball culture. the House of Xtravaganza continues to thrive in the world of ballroom and beyond. As one of the oldest active houses in the ballroom community, the House of Xtravaganza and its members continue to make their impact in the fashion, television, dance, music, and film.

Gloria Evangelina Anzaldua

  • Gloria Evangelina Azaldúa (1942-2004) was a queer Chicana poet, feminisit theorist, and writer. Through her literature and her personal experiences as a Mexican women, Anzaldúa tackles social and cultural marginalization, where she hoped to bring awareness to the injustices minority groups face. Some of her books are Borderlines, Prieta and the Ghost Women, and Light in the Dark. Unfortunately, she died from diabetes, where the disease gave her many complications, but although she is no longer here, her work is still admired today!

Gender Pronouns In Spanish

  • Most gendered words in Spanish end with an “o” or “a” to show if it is feminine or masculine. But, more recently, "x" has been used at the end of words to show inclusivity to those who may not identify with male or female pronouns. Some have trouble pronouncing the "x" so instead of having the “x” at the end, you can also use "e".
  • For example: Latino / Latina / Latinx / Latine
  • They / Them = Elle / Elles
  • Amigo/Amiga = Amige, Amix, Ami, Amigis
  • Non-binary = “Género no binaria” “No binarie”
