How To Be A Good Ally

What is an Ally?

  • An ally is someone who supports and accepts LGBTQ+ people, and advocates for equal rights and fair treatment

Tips on Becoming a Better Ally

  • Stay informed: Get to know differences between certain sexualities and gender identities and stay up to date with news regarding the LGBTQ+ community
  • Speak up: Speaking up for others even though you are not apart of the LGBTQ+ community can help educate others. Correct people when they say offensive words and phrases that are hurtful towards the LGBTQ+ community.
  • Be proud: Make it known that you support and stand with the LGBTQ+ community.
  • Be open: Be open to hear about certain experiences and hardships the LGBTQ+ community faces. Be open to learn about unfamiliar terms regarding the LGBTQ+ community.
  • Don't assume: Don't assume that all of your friends, co-workers, and even family members are straight. Don't assume someone's gender or pronouns. LGBTQ+ people don't look a particular way / ones current / previous partner(s) doesn't define their sexuality.

Why are Allies important to the LGBTQ+ community?

  • Allies are important because it makes people feel like they are supported, accepted and loved by others. They help create a platform for activism to fight homophobia and transphobia
