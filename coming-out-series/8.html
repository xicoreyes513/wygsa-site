Our Stories

Mason

  • There was never one specific moment where I knew I was gay. Rather, it was a string of instances weaving together and forming the realization. I would only be friends with girls. I never showed an interest in stereotypically straight things. I was always questioning whether or not I was actually attracted to a girl or if I was just told that I should be. This went on throughout my life until about 5th grade. It was as if one final piece clicked into place and I realized how inauthentically I had been living my life. This realization was terrifying to me. While my parents had always told me they would love me unconditionally, I couldn't help but fear what they would do if I told them. I can distinctly remember the day I came out. It was 2018 and the Pulse nightclub shooting had occurred only months prior. Thinking about all those people's lives being ended short because of their sexuality was extremely poignant to me. If I don't come out, I could end up dying without ever being to tell anyone. Very melodramatic in retrospect, but the point still stands. On a trip to Target, of all places, I told my mom that I was bisexual. This was not true. I believe subconsciously I wanted to “soften the blow,” so to speak. If they weren't accepting of me dating a guy, they would still have the possibility of dating a girl. My mom's reaction surprised me, she was extremely accepting and comforted me, as I was bawling uncontrollably.

Olivia

  • For the past years I've always questioned myself whether I'm attracted to boys or girls because I've always found myself having crushes and attraction to the same and opposite gender. So I did some research and a lot of questioning and it came down to Bisexual. But I also questioned my gender. At first I thought I identified as a girl/female but there would be a lot of times where I find myself not identifying as any gender and that's when I discovered Non-Binary. The same thing I did for Bisexual I did the same for Non-Binary which was do a lot of research and a lot of questioning. But at one point in life I actually found myself feeling and identifying as a boy, girl. (comfortably) Sometimes I would even fall into the Non-Bianry umbrella and that's when I officially found out I was gender fluid. It felt good to finally find myself and be who I am. If you're wondering, my pronouns are he/him and they/him. There would be a lot of ups and downs questioning whether I'm straight or if I'm really bisexual. But ever since I met this one girl I've had feelings for her for about a while now and I finally had the guts and courage to tell her how I feel. When I noticed that I knew I was bisexual because like I said I've had attractions and crushes for both genders. I came out to my sister, cousin and a couple of friends. I was crying because I was so happy to see the amount of love and support from my friends and family.
