CELEBRITY COMING OUT STORIES

Demi Lovato

  • Demi Lovato discovered their sexuality when they were young as they watched Cruel Intentions, a classic 90's film. Though they knew they were pansexual at a young age, they did not come out till they were 17. Demi first came out as pansexual to their sister and friends when they were 17. Then they came out to the public. “So, there's been moments in my life that have been very confusing to me, you know being 10 years old, attracted to women and not knowing what that meant as a Christian and being raised in the South where that's not very open. You know, it's harder there”. Demi reflected on the experience of coming out to her parents, recalling: “It was actually…emotional but really beautiful”. Then, in the first episode of their new podcast 4D, Lovato says, "Over the past year and a half, I've been doing some healing and self-reflective work. And through this work, I've had the revelation that I identify as nonbinary. With that said, I'll be officially changing my pronouns to they/them” Demi's continues to support and uplift the LGBTQ+ community through music, fundraisers, and awareness. Demi's openness about her coming out process allows others to understand that it is a challenging thing to do, even for a world renowned celebrity.

Ellen Degeneres

  • Ellen DeGeneres officially announced her sexuality to the public on April 14th, 1997 on TV. She appeared on the Oprah Winfrey Show, specifically "The Puppet Episode". During the episode, Ellen is at the airport looking for a woman named Susan, assuming Susan was going to depart someplace else. In the show, the two women had been flirting the day before, and Ellen wanted to tell Susan one last thing before she left. Before telling Susan who she truly is and how she truly feels, Ellen contemplated her decision. Then all of a sudden, she blurts "I'm gay." to Susan, but little did she know the intercom was on, therefore revealing herself to everyone in the airport. This episode then became viral where Ellen was known as the woman who revealed her sexuality on TV. What a clever way to come out! To this day everyone still make references or remembers the way Ellen revealed her true identity .

Halsey

  • Although Halsey never really came out, she has spoke openly about her bisexuality in her video for the 2018 GLAAD awards. In the video she gives her speech for winning the Outstanding Music Artist award, where she nonchalantly mentions how she worked hard to be a better representative for the LGBTQ+ community, specifically for the bisexual friends like her. This video then answers all questions about Halsey sexuality.

Amandla Stenberg

  • Amandla Stenberg is an American actress who is known for her roles in movies such as “The Hate You Give” and “Everything, Everything”. In 2018, Amandla came out as gay. “Gender and sexuality are so fluid—it's okay to change your mind a million times and figure out what works for you. It's okay to take your time… I'd been out as bisexual, and people have known I'm queer for a long time.” Amandla continues to support the LGBTQ+ community with using their platform to talk about being comfortable with your sexuality, and their journey as a gay person.

Elliot Page

  • When Elliot Paige was nine years old, he cut his hair short for the first time. It was exhilarating for him and was one of the first moments of gender euphoria. This was short lived though, as he was required to grow out his hair for an acting job a year later. Now, at 34 years of age, Elliot has come out as trans. He is possibly one of the most famous trans people on the world. He describes the feeling as “true excitement and deep gratitude mixed with a lot of fear and anxiety”
