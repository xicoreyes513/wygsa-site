Lesbian Visibility Week

Hayley Kiyoko

  • American singer, songwriter, dancer and actress.
  • April 3, 1991
  • Since publicly coming out, Kiyoko has been an advocate for LGBT rights and is widely considered to be a gay icon by her fans. Her music videos highlight her own experiences and various LGBT-related issues.
  • “My journey has really been trying to normalize girls loving girls”

Ellen Degeneres

  • American comedian, television host, actress, writer, and producer.
  • January 26, 1958
  • Ellen DeGeneres and her television character came out simultaneously on April 14, 1997. Since, she has advocated for the LGBTQ community from featuring gay couples on her show, to giving a transgender med student $20,000, and to speaking out about homophobia and LGBTQ hate.
  • “It's our challenges and obstacles that give us layers of depth and make us interesting. Are they fun when they happen? No. But they are what make us unique.”

Kate McKinnon

  • American actress, comedian, and writer
  • January 6, 1984
  • Sexuality: Lesbian
  • Kate McKinnon has been very open with her sexuality. She has taken inspiration from Ellen. As LGBTQ+ youth are marginalized in the U.S., McKinnon joins DeGeneres in fighting for inclusion
  • “You've gotta believe in yourself, and you just have to work harder at it than you've ever worked at anything before in your life. And if you keep doing that and keep believing in yourself, great things do happen.”
